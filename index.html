Explore the Cosmos

I think it's safe to say humans are naturally curious creatures. Humans should go to space simply because, not doing so would be a like suppressing natural human instincts. One should consider entertaining the idea of living on an object other than earth, it is likely an evolutionary inevitability.

With bigger developments in rocket and propulsion technologies the price for a ticket to space will decrease and the cosmos will become more accessible to the average american civilian. Eventually civilian astronaut pilots might even exist piloting their own space craft, navigating through space. So why not explore space? The last time there was a space race Americans got all kinds of new technology, all I'm saying is it won't hurt to explore.

Space is Really Really Big...

  • The Sun is one of 200 Billion (or more) stars in the Milky Way galaxy.
  • Earth is one of the eight known planets in The Solar System.
  • The Solar System is one of at least 2,500 planetary systems in the Milky Way galaxy.
  • The Milky Way galaxy is one of at least a hundred billion galaxies in the observable universe.

Defining Terms

Star:
A self-luminous gaseous spheroidal celestial body of great mass which produces energy by means of nuclear fusion reactions
Planet:
A celestial body moving in an elliptical orbit around a star.
Planetary System:
A planetary system is a set of gravitationally bound non-stellar objects in orbit around a star or star system.
Galaxy:
any of the very large groups of stars and associated matter that are found throughout the universe
Observable Universe:
A spherical region of the universe comprising all matter that can be observed from Earth or its space-based telescopes and probes at the present time, because light has had time to reach Earth since the beginning of the cosmological expansion.
